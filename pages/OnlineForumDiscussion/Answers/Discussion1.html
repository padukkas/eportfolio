Discussion 1

Introduction:

Hundreds of LAX flights were delayed or canceled on April 30, 2014, because all computers at the airport crashed due to a bug in their En Route Automation Modernization (ERAM) system. (Storm D, 2014)

Incident:

The system failure was triggered by a U-2 spy plane that flew over southwestern US. The system the entered an endless reboot trying to fix the error, which was due to a lack of altitude information in the airplane’s flight plan. U-2 spy planes normally operate at about 60,000ft, an air traffic controller made an educated guess by tapping in that height. The ERAM system then, it is claimed, started to consider all possible altitudes between “ground level and infinity”, sending the software into a death spiral. (Jasper, 2014)

Cause:

After the air traffic controller entered an estimated altitude of the plane, the system calculated all possible flight paths to ensure that it was not on a crash course with other planes. However, that process caused the system to fall short in memory and shut down every other flight processing function.

Impact:

As a result of this system failure thousands of flights were delayed leaving customers stranded at the LAX airport. Fortunately, no lived were harmed during this incident but it could have turned been a live costing incident, therefore more testing should have been done.
