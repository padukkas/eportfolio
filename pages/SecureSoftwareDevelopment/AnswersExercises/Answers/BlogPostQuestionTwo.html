Blog Post: Question 2

Companies tend to overlook the threat from their employees due to trust. These malicious actions could be motivated by money, intent to hurt the company and its reputation or simply not enough security training. Up to 28% of Enterprise Data Security Incidents Come from Inside (Virtru, 2015).

  • Authentication – This will allow the company verify the identity of the user. There are three common factors that can be used to authenticate a user. Something you know such as a password or a pin, something you have such as a smart card or an access token, and something you are would be your biometric identity (fingerprint, facial recognition)
  • Authenticity – The employee should be able prove they are who they claim to be
  • Access control – This plays a major role in security aspect of a company. Access control is a security technique that regulates who or what can view or use resources in a computing environment (Lutkevich, 2020). This type of control will minimize a threat since only the employees who are allowed to see certain data sets can see and access said data.
  • Audit – This practice will ensure the employees are following the security practices, perform vulnerability testing and also make sure practices such as access control, authentication and etc. are being followed.
  • Monitoring – Continuous monitoring keeps everyone involved honest. Also, this will help catch threats and vulnerabilities ahead of time before they cause major damage to systems.

    • Knowing the above mentioned one policy I recommend is the Zero Trust policy where it requires all users, whether in or outside the organization's network, to be authenticated, authorized, and continuously validated for security configuration and posture before being granted or keeping access to applications and data. (Raina, 2021)
