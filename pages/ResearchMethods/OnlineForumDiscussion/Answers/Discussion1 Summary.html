Discussion 1: Codes of Ethics and Professional Conduct - Summary Post

In collaborative discussion 1 under the initial post, I discussed the given case study of abusive workplace behavior while giving a much further analysis on what amounts to such behaviors along with some effective remedies.

When we further look into the given workplace scenario, it is far more evident that having an unhealthy workplace environment violates the basic principles of the referred Code of Conduct. Such as, Principle 1.a - “Securing the public interest and the wellbeing of others and the environment” along with Principle 2.f, “Avoiding injuring others reputation...” and Principle 3.b “Avoiding situations that conflicts of interests might can arise” etc.

In his book “Dying for a Paycheck,” Professor Jeffery Pfeffer of Stanford university, points out modern workplaces as the central reason behind unhealthy mental issues among many working people; which also leads to unhealthy household environments. Therefore, according to his studies, having a healthy workplace environment can have a positive impact on one's family and household as well (Gillian Mohney, 2018).

Further, professor Pfeffer explains the role behind organizations and companies CEO’s and Directors hold when it comes to their employee's psychological wellbeing. He highlights that fact with the following quote.

“Your boss is more important for your health than your family doctor.”

When we analyze all the above information it is far more evident that just having a Code of Conduct is not effective for the employees if there is no firm bord of people behind the code to make the given policies practically effective by giving meaning to the code through providing reporting systems, counselling options, team-based work experiences and activities, social events etc. to build up a positive and healthy bond between employees. (Gillian Mohney, 2018).
