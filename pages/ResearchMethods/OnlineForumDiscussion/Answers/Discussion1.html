Discussion 1: Codes of Ethics and Professional Conduct

Discussion Topic: Codes of Ethics and Professional Conduct

There are disagreements in every relationship but none of them needs to be handled in an aggressive or abusive way. It is a common fact when it comes to workplaces the hierarchies that people hold tend to make them believe that they are superior than some employees and that they are entitled to use force on them. These behaviors are not just verbal every time, there are numerous ways that some employees get bullied or harassed in nonverbal ways such as, ignorance, body movements, aggressive facial expressions, unnatural glaring, and even silent treatments (Alder, 1989). When we further look into these behaviors, having low self-esteem, low job satisfaction, personal issues resulting depression and stress can result in making an abusive person who hates to aid or compliment fellow employees (Chen & Spector, 1991).

When analyzing the given scenario, it clearly highlights how an unhealthy workplace environment violates the basic principles of the referred Code of Conduct. From the very first principle, Principle 1.a - “Securing the public interest and the wellbeing of others and the environment” along with Principle 2.f, “Avoiding injuring others reputation...” and Principle 3.b “Avoiding situations that conflicts of interests might can arise” etc.

In my opinion, Workplace harassment or bullying cannot be completely wiped out, since every workplace consists of different people with diverse backgrounds and beliefs, but it can be controlled by having a firm code of conduct and workplace policies and a reporting system along with counseling options, having monthly reviews and employee assistance programs etc. (Healthline, 2019)

Further, by reminding ourselves that being bullied is not our fault but their own is the best way to avoid the negative effect of being bullied.

On the other hand, not every workplace disagreement is harassment, for a workplace to function effectively there should be objective and constructive criticism as well.
