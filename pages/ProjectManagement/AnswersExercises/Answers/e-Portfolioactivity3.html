Question: Review the article by McCall, Richards & Walters (1977). Select a timelier academic article on software quality. Discuss, in 300 words, the major differences in relation to software quality between the two articles.

Answer

When we analyze the given article named “Factors in software quality-concept and definition of software quality” by McCall, Richards & Walters with another timely article on software quality titled, “Software quality: definitions and strategic issues” by Ronan Fitzpatrick, even though both articles define software quality concept, the approaches and the focus areas are noticeably differ from one another. Those differences can be briefed as follows,

Even though the article by McCall, Richards & Walter takes an in-depth global approach it limits the defined quality factors to Air Force applications while the article by Ronan Fitzpatrick takes a general approach focusing on instances where quality influences an organization's strategic philosophy.

Further, unlike the former article, the article by Ronan Fitzpatrick analyzes quality factors by subdividing it into two subcategories namely, External quality factors and internal quality factors. Here factors that are clearly recognizable to the end users are defined under external factors while internal factors are analyzed under technical issues relating to the software.

The article by McCall, Richards & Walter evaluates an in-depth analysis on factors relating to software quality focusing on System Program Office (SPO) directed concept along with formation and application of suitable metrics. Further, McCall, Richards & Walter categorizes metrics into two subcategories; first being “relative quality measure” and the second being “binary measure”- which ascertain the existence or nonattendance of a particular thing while the later article by Ronan Fitzpatrick being silent on measures of those attributes.

The idea of behavioral design centers on how a system or structure, as seen by the users, satisfies their wants and necessities. The key to a good behavioral design works like a lock and a key. Here, the product is the key, and customers and their behaviors are the lock. When the two are in perfect harmony, everything is in perfect order. (Philips, 2017)

In conclusion, when we analyze the contents of both of these articles, while evaluating the aforementioned differences, it is evident that, even though there are some considerable differences connected to the focus areas of these two reports, both the articles are commonly rich in defining factors relating to software quality such as, reusability, efficiency, portability, accuracy, flexibility, transferability etc.
