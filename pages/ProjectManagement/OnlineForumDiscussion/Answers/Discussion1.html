Discussion 1: Project Failures Study

Question 1: What do you believe are the three most common reasons for project failure?

It is known that not every project gets successfully completed. With a project there are lots of individuals, tasks and opinions involved which means there are multiple points of failure. There are lots of reasons why some projects fail and in opinion the following three are on top of that list,

  • Lack of software testing (QA)
  • Poor decision making
  • Lack of risk management

    • Question 2: Give two examples of failures that support your choices (there are several examples in the lecturecast).

    Example 1: Boeing 737 Max accident.

    The fatal Boeing 737 Max accident took the lives of 346 passengers and crew (Cusumano, 2021). This accident was not just a technical failure but a management failure as well. This all started with Boeings decision to put larger and more fuel-efficient engines on older aircrafts, the new engines significantly changed the pitch angle and stability of the older 737. In order to cut costs instead of redesigning the plane, Boeing chose to install MCAS, which was adapted from another aircraft and the idea was that it will be able to emulate and adapt the newly installed components.

    This type of poor decision making by the whole company costs people their lives, which is unacceptable.

    Example 2: The IBM OS/360 Project

    IBM 360 was a project taken on by IBM to develop a single OS that would run on all mainframes manufactured by IBM (textbooks.cs.ksu.edu, n.d.). While developing the program the developers ran into the problem of running more than one program at a time to which they hired more than 1000 programmers to solve the issue which cost IBM a fortune. They were able to deliver to the program a month late and far over buget.

    In my opinion this was caused due to multiple factors, including poor project management, poor decision making, poor communication and many more.
