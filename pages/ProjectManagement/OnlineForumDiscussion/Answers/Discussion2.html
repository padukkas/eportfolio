Discussion 2: Factors Affecting User Experience

Introduction:

In general, as humans we experience a wide range of emotions in our day today life. Happiness, when we accomplished a targeted goal, sadness when we failed to reach our goals, frustration, when we are striving to achieve our goals; likewise, every step that we take, every move that we make, every action is based on emotions. Same with when we interact with technology and related products; from the visual aspect to the use and function we experience numerous emotions. This aspect is known as “User Experience (UX).”

When it comes to product-related emotions, this can be subdivided into “initial stage” and “later stage.” In the initial stage, prior to any interaction, perceived usability is affected by the device's visual appeal. And after a brief interaction with the product, in the later stage, the prior visual appeal fades, and perceived emotions and visual appeal begin to be influenced by system usability (Minge and Thüring, 2018).

These two effects can be identified as,

“Hedonic halo effect (attractive is usable)” and “Pragmatic halo effect (usable gets attractive).” (Minge and Thüring, 2018)

As for an example, the well-known search engine “Google” can be highlighted; the use of its visual image- well-balanced webpage, use of colors and spaces, clear search box area attract the users to interact with the product itself; and after a short-term interaction, users tend to visit the webpage more frequently because of its fast, well-built, and structured supply of data. (Dawson, 2019)
